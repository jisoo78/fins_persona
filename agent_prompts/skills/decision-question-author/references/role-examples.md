# Role Examples

Examples show how to select attributes and interpretations. Do not copy them as fixed templates.

## CFO

Category: Investment decision criteria

Possible attributes:

- Payback period
- Downside exposure
- Cash runway impact
- Expected return
- Strategic value

Useful interpretations:

- Limits downside before pursuing upside.
- Accepts volatility for strategic or return upside.
- Balances return, risk, and liquidity.
- Protects runway even if growth slows.

## CTO

Category: Architecture or delivery decision criteria

Possible attributes:

- Reliability risk
- Delivery speed
- Technical debt
- Reversibility
- Operational complexity
- Team learning value

Useful interpretations:

- Protects reliability before launch speed.
- Accepts temporary debt for market timing.
- Chooses reversible experiments over large commitments.
- Prioritizes long-term maintainability over short-term delivery.

## CMO

Category: Campaign and brand decision criteria

Possible attributes:

- CAC efficiency
- Brand risk
- Learning value
- Reach
- Conversion confidence
- Channel dependency

Useful interpretations:

- Protects brand trust before short-term growth.
- Pays for learning when uncertainty is high.
- Optimizes measurable conversion over reach.
- Diversifies channel risk even at lower efficiency.

## CEO

Category: Strategic and organizational decision criteria

Possible attributes:

- Strategic control
- Market timing
- Organizational disruption
- Capital intensity
- Talent focus
- Reversibility

Useful interpretations:

- Moves early when market timing matters.
- Preserves organizational stability before expansion.
- Keeps strategic control even at higher cost.
- Prefers reversible bets until conviction is high.

## Stage Fit Examples

`preference`:

- Ask the default direction in a realistic scenario.
- Attribute trade-off can work if default criteria are too abstract.

`context_shift`:

- Change a constraint and ask whether the decision changes.
- Usually scenario choice, not a table.

`core_value`:

- Ask what the user refuses to sacrifice.
- Usually scenario choice or boundary check.

`red_line`:

- Ask for a stop condition.
- Usually boundary check.

`priority_order`:

- Ask what wins when two or more criteria collide.
- Attribute trade-off can work when ranking alone is too shallow.
